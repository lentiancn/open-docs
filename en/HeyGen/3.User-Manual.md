# HeyGen User Manual

## Video Content Creation Tips

### Script Writing Best Practices

High-quality video content starts with excellent scripts. When writing scripts in HeyGen, the following recommendations will help you achieve the best results:

First, keep script content concise and clear. Avoid using overly long or complex sentences, as AI digital avatars maintain natural pauses when speaking, and overly long sentences may cause the video rhythm to drag. Professional terminology, names, and place names can be manually adjusted using the platform's pronunciation correction function to ensure accurate AI voiceover.

Second, make full use of punctuation to help the system understand emotional expression. Exclamation marks can increase the digital avatar's emotional investment, question marks can create a questioning tone, and periods indicate statement endings. Adding appropriate modifiers before important information can enhance the video's persuasiveness.

Additionally, adjust script style according to the target audience. Content for younger audiences can use more lively language, while content for professional audiences should maintain a formal tone. HeyGen's digital avatars will automatically adjust expressions and body language based on script style, and matching content will produce better viewing experiences.

### Video Rhythm Control

The overall rhythm of a video directly affects viewer experience and completion rates. Here are some practical rhythm control recommendations:

It is recommended to keep individual videos between 1-3 minutes. Overly long videos reduce viewer completion rates, especially on social media platforms. If you need to convey a lot of information, it is recommended to split the content into multiple short video series, with each video focusing on one core message point.

The first 5 seconds are most critical and need to quickly capture viewer attention. This can be achieved by asking questions, showing eye-catching visuals, or making commitments. Core content should be presented in the first half of the video to ensure key information is delivered even if viewers leave halfway through.

The ending should include a clear Call to Action (CTA), guiding viewers to take the next step. This could be subscribing to the channel, visiting a website, purchasing products, or engaging in interaction.

## Multilingual Content Production

### International Content Strategy

HeyGen's multilingual support is a powerful tool for creating international content. Here are some best practices:

When creating source content, use clear, simple language. Simpler source language produces more accurate translations. Avoid using slang, puns, or culture-specific references, as these elements may lose their original meaning during translation.

Use HeyGen's translation function to quickly generate multilingual versions. Translated videos retain the original speaker's voice characteristics and expression style, ensuring brand consistency. With support for over 175 languages, it covers nearly all major global markets.

### Translation Quality Optimization

Although AI translation is already quite accurate, some optimizations can further improve quality:

Carefully check original script grammar before translation. Grammar errors may cause inaccurate translations.

After translation completes, Pro version users can use editing and proofreading functions to fine-tune translation scripts. This is especially important for professional terminology and brand names.

The same brand may require different expressions in different language markets. It is recommended to appropriately adjust scripts according to target market cultural habits rather than simply relying on machine translation.

## Team Collaboration

### Workflow Optimization

For teams using HeyGen, it is recommended to establish a clear workflow:

Content Planning Phase: Determine video goals, target audiences, and core messages, and develop a content calendar.
Script Creation Phase: Write and review scripts to ensure accuracy and brand alignment.
Video Production Phase: Create videos in HeyGen, selecting avatars, voices, and backgrounds.
Review and Publishing Phase: Team members review videos, make modifications, and export for publishing.

Use project management functions to organize video content. Create different project folders to categorize related videos. Projects support adding descriptions and tags for easy retrieval and reuse later.

### Role and Permission Management

Business plans provide fine-grained role control functions. Administrators can set permissions for different roles, including:

Super Administrator: Full access including account settings and billing management.
Administrator: Manage team members and content access permissions.
Creator: Create and edit videos, but cannot manage team members.
Reviewer: Can only view and comment on videos, cannot edit or export.

Content access control can be set to Public (visible to all team members), Private (only visible to creators and administrators), or specific member visibility, meeting different confidentiality level requirements.

## Video Quality Optimization

### Resolution and Format Selection

Choosing the correct resolution and format is crucial for video quality:

720p (HD): Suitable for social media platform uploads, smaller file size, faster loading.
1080p (Full HD): Suitable for most scenarios, good balance between clarity and file size.
4K (Ultra HD): Suitable for large-screen display or professional occasion use, larger file size.

For format selection, MP4 is the most compatible format, playable on almost all platforms and devices. If needed for specific platforms, you can choose MOV (suitable for Apple ecosystem) or AVI formats.

### Generation Speed Optimization

Video generation speed is affected by multiple factors, and the following tips can optimize wait times:

Avoid peak hours. Weekends and off-peak hours typically have lower server load.
Use paid plans. Creator and Pro plans offer faster video processing.
Simplify video elements. Excessive scene transitions and complex effects increase processing time.
Batch create tasks can submit multiple videos at once, and the system will process them in sequence.

### Common Issue Troubleshooting

If video generation fails or effects are unsatisfactory, try the following solutions:

Check network connection. Stable network is fundamental for video generation.
Verify account quota. Tasks beyond monthly quota will be queued.
Review content compliance. Non-compliant content will be intercepted by the system.
Try simplifying scripts. Overly complex content may cause processing failures.

## Advanced Tips

### Digital Avatar Expression Control

HeyGen's digital avatars support rich expression control. You can precisely control digital avatar emotional expression by adding expression prompt words in the script:

Use "said with a smile" or "happily" when expressing happiness.
Use "seriously" or "earnestly" when expressing seriousness.
Use "confused" or "puzzled" when questioning.

Digital avatar gestures can also be controlled through text prompts, such as "waving arm" or "nodding".

### Batch Production Strategy

For scenarios requiring large numbers of similar videos, you can use templates and batch functions:

Create a basic video template with fixed backgrounds, avatars, and brand elements.
Save as a reusable template to quickly create new videos.
Use API for automated batch production.

For regular content (like weekly reports, monthly updates), establishing standardized production processes can significantly improve efficiency.

## Data Analytics

### Effect Tracking

Although HeyGen doesn't provide video playback analytics itself, you can combine third-party tools:

Add UTM tracking parameters in video descriptions.
Use short link services to track click sources.
Integrate analytics tools on landing pages.

Through this data, you can understand audience preferences for video content and optimize subsequent content strategies.
