# Introduction

Jira is a project management and issue tracking tool developed by Atlassian, launched in 2002. Over time, it has become one of the most widely used project management platforms globally, with more than 300,000 companies adopting Jira.

## Core Purpose

Jira serves as a single source of truth for organizations, bringing every team together to plan, track, and deliver any type of project with confidence. It empowers teams with the context they need to move quickly while staying connected to greater business goals. Whether managing everyday tasks or scaling complex projects, Jira is where teams and AI come together to drive better outcomes.

## Product Lines

Jira offers several products to meet different team needs:

**Jira Software** is designed for software development teams, supporting agile methodologies with Scrum boards, Kanban boards, and other agile tools. It helps software teams manage projects and deliver software through iterative development.

**Jira Service Management** serves IT service management and customer service teams, providing request management, incident handling, and service desk capabilities.

**Jira Align** is designed for large enterprises, helping organizations with portfolio management and cross-team collaboration at the strategic level.

## Key Features

Jira provides multiple view types to meet different project management needs. List views are suitable for viewing and editing large numbers of issues. Kanban boards visualize workflow by displaying issues as cards across different columns, allowing teams to see task status at a glance. Timeline views work well for planning large amounts of work months in advance within a single project, with dependency management features. Calendar views help teams track milestones and deadlines.

The platform supports powerful workflow customization, allowing users to define issue status transitions, approval processes, and automation rules based on business requirements. With Rovo AI, teams can plan projects more efficiently, automate repetitive tasks, and gain data-driven insights.

## Target Teams

Jira's flexibility enables cross-functional teams to collaborate effectively, including software teams, marketing teams, project management teams, product management teams, design teams, IT teams, and operations teams. The platform offers an extensive template library covering scenarios like software development, marketing, human resources, and sales, helping teams launch projects quickly.

## Deployment Options

Jira offers both cloud and on-premises deployment options. The cloud version is hosted and maintained by Atlassian, requiring no infrastructure management. The Data Center version is for enterprises with strict regulatory or security requirements, supporting self-managed deployment.
